Rules of use

Use AI safely and professionally.

AI is useful, but it still needs judgement, context and review.

Do not shareReviewToneContext

Do not share

Never share passwords, credentials, API keys, MFA codes, security-sensitive information, confidential client information, sensitive company data, unpublished financials, personal employee data or anything you would not be comfortable sending outside approved systems.

Review before use

Check all facts, numbers, prices, dates, vehicle details, claims, legal wording and anything customer-facing. AI output should be treated as a draft, not a final authority.

Keep the tone professional

Use AI to make communication clearer and more helpful. Avoid exaggerated claims, overpromising, aggressive wording or anything that does not sound like KGM Australia.

Give useful context

Good context includes department, audience, purpose, source material, constraints, tone and output format. Poor context creates vague answers.

Bad: Write me something about Musso.
Better: Write a 150-word dealer email about Musso for ABN buyers. Tone: practical and confident. Mention value and warranty, but do not invent prices.
