Read before using AI

Use AI safely, clearly and professionally.

The rules are simple: protect sensitive information, give context, check outputs and keep the KGM tone.

Do not shareReviewBrand toneContextDecisions

Do not share sensitive information

Never share passwords, credentials, API keys, MFA codes, security-sensitive information, confidential client information, sensitive company data, unpublished financials, personal employee data, full VIN/customer datasets, or anything that should only live in approved company systems.

Review before use

Check all facts, numbers, prices, dates, vehicle details, warranty claims, legal wording, source links and anything customer-facing. AI output is a draft, not an authority.

Keep the tone professional and on-brand

Yes, this rule has a real point. AI often overdoes marketing language: “game-changing”, “unbeatable”, “guaranteed”, “revolutionary”, or wording that sounds too aggressive or too American. Use AI to make communication clearer, more useful and easier to understand — not louder. For KGM Australia, aim for practical, confident, helpful and factual.

Instead of: This incredible offer will destroy the competition.
Better: This offer gives dealers a practical value message for ABN buyers, supported by warranty and clear ownership benefits.

Give useful context

The chat cannot read your mind. It does not know what report you are looking at, who Leigh is, which dealer you mean, what “urgent” means in your team, or what tone sounds normal for KGM unless you explain it.

Bad: Write me something about Musso.
Better: Write a 150-word dealer email about Musso for ABN buyers. Tone: practical and confident. Mention value and warranty, but do not invent prices. Use Australian English.

You make the decision

AI can summarise, draft and suggest. It should not approve payments, make warranty decisions, invent policy, commit pricing, or decide commercial strategy without human review.
