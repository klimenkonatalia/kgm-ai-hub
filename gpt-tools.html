Which model should I use?

Use caseRecommended choice
Quick emails, rewrites, simple summariesInstant / fast mode
Analysis, strategy, complex comparison, messy dataThinking / higher reasoning
Long research with sourcesDeep research
Image concepts or editsImage generation/editing tool

Use the simplest model that does the job. Move to a stronger reasoning mode when the task has more moving parts, ambiguity or risk.

Instant, medium, high: plain English

Think of these as effort levels. Instant is fast and good for everyday drafting. Medium is a balanced default for tasks with some reasoning. High is for harder work where you want more careful analysis, but it may take longer and may use more of your available allowance.

Useful GPT tools

Deep research

Use for complex online research where you need a documented report with sources, not a quick answer.

File uploads

Use for spreadsheets, PDFs, meeting notes and reports. Ask for trends, summaries, checks or action lists.

Canvas

Use when drafting or editing a longer piece of writing and you want to iterate cleanly.

Image generation

Use for concept visuals, campaign directions and mockups. Review carefully before using externally.

Projects

Use to keep related chats, files and instructions together for a longer piece of work.

GPTs

Use custom GPTs for repeatable internal workflows with saved instructions and examples.

Limits: what to know

Some tools and models have usage limits. If you hit a limit, switch to a lighter model, wait for the limit to reset, or use the tool for higher-value tasks first. Deep research and image generation are more limited than normal chat, so use them when they genuinely add value.

Safe use reminder

Business plans are designed with stronger business data protections, but internal policy still matters. Do not share passwords, credentials, security-sensitive information, confidential client information or sensitive company data unless it has been approved for that use.
