How to get useful answers from AI.

Start with context, ask for a clear output, then review and refine.

MindsetContextPrompt formulaKeep goingReview

Think of AI as a smart new employee

ChatGPT and Claude are fast and useful, but they do not automatically understand KGM Australia, your department, the dealer network, internal reports, current priorities or what you already know. Treat AI like someone capable who has just joined the business: helpful, but missing context until you provide it.

AI cannot read your mind

If the request is vague, the answer will usually be vague. The chat cannot climb into your brain and guess the audience, purpose, constraints, tone, source material or internal background. You need to give it the situation.

Bad prompts make AI guess. Good prompts make AI work with your actual task.

Give backgroundDepartment, market, model, dealer/customer type, and why the task matters.
Give the audienceDealer, customer, GM, finance team, technical team, supplier or internal staff.
Give constraintsLength, tone, format, must-include points, what not to mention or invent.
Give source materialPaste notes, upload a file, or provide the data you want it to use.

A simple prompt formula

Role/context: I work in [department] at KGM Australia.
Task: Help me [do the thing].
Audience: This is for [dealer/customer/manager/team].
Source material: Use the notes/data below.
Constraints: Keep it under [length], use Australian English, do not invent facts.
Output: Return it as [email/table/checklist/summary/slides].

Do not stop after the first answer

The first answer is a draft. Ask follow-up questions: make it shorter, make it more practical, add a table, focus on dealers, use a less salesy tone, give risks, or explain assumptions.

Review before use

AI can be confidently wrong. Check facts, figures, pricing, vehicle details, source links and anything customer-facing. Use it to speed up thinking and drafting, not to replace judgement.
